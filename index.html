Sarah Seiyuen Kim is an experience designer and multimedia artist shaping the aesthetics of human-machine interaction. Through new media technologies and critical research methods, her work investigates time, memory, and cultural identity from perspectives of the contemporary cultural moment.

She will complete a B.S. in Integrated Design and Media at NYU Tandon School of Engineering in May 2025, with a minor in Media, Culture, and Communication. She is currently exploring how computation transforms human emotion, thought, and behavior, through algorithmic art and examinations of the human psyche within social, environmental, and conceptual ecologies.

Her technical practice is grounded in a formal education in design fundamentals. She is experienced in graphic design, sound design, live signal processing, film production, projection and installation design, interaction design, front- and back-end development, circuit design, and more. Working on individual and collaborative projects, she has developed a dynamic process that understands both personal and shared visions.

At the heart of her practice is the age-old tradition of storytelling. Stories connect us across generations and cultures, both reminding and reimagining collective presences. She constructs meaningful narratives by designing sensory experiences inspired to nourish cultural memory, spirit, and functioning. Her senior thesis, an interactive sound installation, explores sound, indeterminacy, and consciousness, reimagining identities beyond colonial frameworks.

Among other things, Sarah is an impressionist artist, experimental and classically trained cellist, writer, composer, and learner of new languages. She is inspired by phantasmagoria, natural patterns, and vanishing points. She is curious about semantics, cybernetics, natural patterns and is endlessly enchanted by the mysteries of life~
