Sarah Kim is an experiential designer and multimedia artist shaping the aesthetics of human-machine interaction. Through technical mediums, her work investigates time, memory, and cultural identity from the perspective of the contemporary civilizational moment.

She will complete a B.S. in Integrated Design and Media at NYU Tandon School of Engineering in Spring 2025, with a minor in Media, Culture, and Communication. Her design experience spans multimedia projection and installation, graphic and sound design, film production, experience and interaction design, and front-end web development.

Sarah questions the boundaries of perceived reality, exploring how technology and media shape our understanding of self and the world. Her works intentionally engage the senses in altered states to emphasize their malleability and to challenge participants' perceptions of time, space, and identity. Through this exploration, she connects to the broader themes of her practice, examining how technological advancement influences cultural narratives and personal agency. As society becomes increasingly entwined with digital networks and media manipulation, Sarah’s work serves as both a critique and reflection on the contemporary civilizational moment, urging us to reconsider the forces that shape our collective consciousness.

At the heart of Sarah’s practice is the tradition of storytelling– she believes in the power of stories to connect us across generations and cultures. Stories remind us of our origin from the Earth and of the forces that interconnect us all. Sarah creates spaces that resonate with the complexities of the present through emerging technologies, nourishing collective memory and the human spirit.

Among other things, Sarah is an impressionist artist, experimental and classically trained cellist, writer, composer, and learner of new languages. She is inspired by phantasmagoria, natural patterns, and vanishing points. She is curious about semantics, cybernetics, natural patterns and is endlessly enchanted by the mysteries of life~
